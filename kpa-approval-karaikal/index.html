Why Building Plan Approval Is Non-Negotiable

Four Consequences of
Building Without Approval

KPA approval is not bureaucratic paperwork. It is legal protection for your investment, your family, and the transaction value of the property you are building.

01 ⚖

Legal Compliance

Construction without an approved KPA plan violates the Puducherry Town and Country Planning Act. The Karaikal Planning Authority has the authority to issue stop-work notices and demolition orders for unapproved structures. A building that begins without approval can be ordered to be stopped or demolished at any stage — including after completion.

02 🔌

Utility Connections

The CEPC (electricity) and TWAD (water) departments in Karaikal require a copy of the KPA-approved building plan before granting new connections. A completed house without KPA approval cannot get an electricity connection or a water supply connection. No approval means no utilities — regardless of how well the house is built.

03 🏦

Home Loan Disbursement

Banks and housing finance companies require the KPA-approved building plan as part of the loan documentation. If you are financing your construction through a bank loan, the disbursement of funds at each construction stage typically requires the approved plan. Without it, loan payments stop regardless of your repayment history.

04 📈

Future Resale Value

A property without KPA approval is legally encumbered. Any future sale, mortgage, or transfer will be complicated or impossible — buyers and their legal advisors will identify the missing approval during due diligence. The long-term value of your property depends on it being legally constructed in accordance with the approved plan from the start.

Why Plans Get Rejected

Common Mistakes That
Delay Your Approval

01
Common Mistake

Incorrect Setback Distances

The proposed building is drawn too close to the plot boundary, road margin, or neighbouring structure. KPA regulations specify minimum setbacks on all four sides depending on road width and plot size. A plan with incorrect setbacks is returned immediately.

How KTR Builders Prevents It

Er. Sasi Kumar measures the plot, identifies the road width, and applies the current KPA setback schedule before any drawing begins. The site plan reflects actual measured dimensions — not assumed ones from a patta document that may have been surveyed decades earlier.

02
Common Mistake

Missing or Incomplete Structural Drawings

KPA requires structural drawings — column layout, foundation details, beam sections — signed by a registered engineer. Many plan preparers submit architectural drawings only, without the structural component. The plan is returned for missing documentation.

How KTR Builders Prevents It

As a Chartered Engineer, Er. Sasi Kumar prepares both the architectural and structural drawings as a single integrated submission. There is no outsourcing of structural drawings — the engineer who stamps them designed them.

03
Common Mistake

FSI (Floor Space Index) Violations

The total built-up area proposed exceeds the permissible Floor Space Index for the plot size and zone. This is a common error when floor plan designs are done without verifying the applicable FSI limits in the KPA regulations for that specific area.

How KTR Builders Prevents It

Before the floor plan is designed, Er. Sasi Kumar determines the plot's zone classification and the applicable FSI. The total built-up area is checked against the permissible limit before drawings are finalised — not after the client has approved a design that cannot be approved by KPA.

04
Common Mistake

Site Measurement Errors

The dimensions in the site plan do not match the actual plot — because the preparers copied dimensions from a patta document without physically measuring the plot. Discrepancies between the patta and the actual site are common in older plots across Karaikal district.

How KTR Builders Prevents It

Er. Sasi Kumar or a supervised team physically measures the plot before drawing begins. In plots where patta dimensions differ from actual measurements, the discrepancy is flagged and the appropriate process to reconcile the records is followed before the plan is submitted.

05
Common Mistake

Incomplete Land Document Package

The submission is missing one or more required documents — encumbrance certificate, up-to-date chitta, or survey sketch. A submission with a missing document is returned without review. The clock resets.

How KTR Builders Prevents It

After the initial consultation, clients receive a precise document checklist for their specific plot type. Er. Sasi Kumar reviews all documents before submission to confirm completeness. A plan is only submitted when every required document is in hand — not optimistically, hoping a missing item can be added later.

02
Site Verification

Physical Measurement & Boundary Confirmation

The plot is physically measured — all four boundaries, road frontage, and surrounding context. Existing structures on or near the plot are noted. If plot dimensions differ from patta records (common in older Karaikal plots in areas like Keezhakasakudy and Thirunallar), the discrepancy is documented and addressed before the site plan is drawn.

The site plan reflects what exists on the ground — not what a document from a different decade says should be there.
03
Drawing Preparation

Architectural & Structural Drawings to KPA Standard

Er. Sasi Kumar prepares the complete drawing set required by KPA: site plan showing setbacks and plot context, floor plan for each floor, elevations (front, rear, side), cross-section, and structural drawings (foundation layout, column schedule, beam details). All drawings are signed and stamped by the KPA Registered Engineer.

One engineer prepares all drawings. Architectural and structural components are coordinated — not produced by two separate professionals who have never spoken.
04
Documentation

Complete Document Package Assembly

The full document package is assembled: drawings, land documents (patta, chitta, adangal), encumbrance certificate, survey sketch, application forms, and engineer certificate. Every item on the KPA submission checklist is physically verified before the application is prepared. Nothing is submitted optimistically.

A complete submission on day one is the only way to avoid a return on day thirty.
05
Submission

KPA Submission by the Registered Engineer

Er. K. Sasi Kumar submits the plan to the Karaikal Planning Authority as the engineer of record. Submission fees are paid, acknowledgement is obtained, and the file number is tracked. If the KPA raises a query or requests a minor clarification, it is addressed promptly — without restarting the process from scratch.

Er. Sasi Kumar is the point of contact with the KPA office. You do not need to visit the office, follow up on file status, or manage any part of this process.
06
Approval & Handover

Approved Plan Received — Construction Can Begin

Once the KPA issues the approval order, you receive the stamped and approved building plan. Er. Sasi Kumar walks through the approved drawings with you — confirming what is approved and any conditions noted by the KPA. Construction commences only against the approved plan. No deviations from the approved drawings are made during construction.

Approval typically takes 30–60 days from a complete, compliant first submission. With 250+ approved plans, the process is understood in detail — not learned at your expense.

What KPA Registration Means for Your Building Plan

KPA Registered Engineers are professionals who have been individually registered by the Karaikal Planning Authority as qualified to prepare and sign building plan submissions on behalf of plot owners. Registration requires recognised engineering qualifications and professional standing — it is not a licence available to anyone.

When Er. K. Sasi Kumar's registration number appears on your building plan, it means a recognised professional engineer has verified that the drawings comply with current KPA regulations and has staked his professional reputation on that verification. If the plan has errors, his registration is at risk — which is why the plan does not have errors.

A building plan prepared by someone without KPA registration cannot be submitted to the KPA. Plans prepared by non-engineers are returned immediately. Using a KPA Registered Engineer is not optional — it is the only legal path to getting a plan approved.

Residential · G+1 · Kottucherry

Patta Dimensions vs. Actual Site

Kottucherry, Karaikal · 2022

Challenge

Client had a patta showing a 40×60 ft plot. Physical measurement revealed the actual plot was 38×58 ft due to historical encroachments on one boundary. The proposed G+1 design, based on patta dimensions, would violate setbacks when drawn on the actual site.

What Was Done

The discrepancy was identified during site measurement — before any drawing was prepared. The floor plan was adjusted for the actual plot dimensions while maintaining the client's room requirements. KPA submission prepared on actual site dimensions.

Outcome

Plan approved in 44 days. No return for correction. Construction commenced on time. If drawn on patta dimensions and submitted, the plan would have been returned — adding 2–3 months to the timeline.

Commercial · G+1 · Thirunallar

FSI Limit & Design Adjustment

Thirunallar, Karaikal · 2023

Challenge

Client wanted a G+1 commercial building with a total built-up area exceeding the permissible FSI for the plot size and zone. An architect had already prepared a design that the client liked — but the design could not be approved as drawn.

What Was Done

Er. Sasi Kumar calculated the permissible FSI and worked with the client to prioritise the spaces most essential to the commercial use. The ground floor plan was reorganised, and the first-floor area adjusted to bring total built-up within the permissible limit without compromising the core business requirement.

Outcome

Revised plan approved in 52 days. The client's business opened on schedule. The original design, if submitted, would have been returned — requiring a redesign under time pressure.

Renovation · Neravy · Karaikal

Extension on Unapproved Existing Structure

Neravy, Karaikal · 2023

Challenge

Client wanted to add a first floor to an existing ground-floor home built in the 1990s without KPA approval. The extension required KPA approval for the new floor — but the existing structure also needed to be regularised and reflected correctly in the submissions.

What Was Done

Er. Sasi Kumar assessed whether the existing structure could support the proposed addition. As-built drawings of the existing structure were prepared. The KPA submission included both the existing ground-floor regularisation and the proposed first-floor addition, handled as a single coordinated application.

Outcome

Both approvals obtained. The extended home is now legally approved at both floors. The client's existing ground floor is also regularised — protecting the property's resale and legal standing.
