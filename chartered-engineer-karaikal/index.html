What Is a Chartered Engineer — and Why It Matters for Your Building

Chartered Engineer (CE) status is awarded by the Institution of Engineers India to engineers who meet rigorous criteria: an accredited B.E. degree, proven professional experience, competency assessment, and commitment to ongoing professional development. The IEI was founded in 1920 and is India’s premier engineering professional body. CE status is a regulated professional designation — not a self-declared qualification.

For homeowners, this means one thing: the engineer supervising your project has been formally assessed as competent. His name is on the IEI register. If he issues a structural certificate or signs a drawing, that signature carries professional accountability — not just company liability.
