What Changes When Your Interior Designer Is the Engineer

01

No Structural Surprises

The biggest interior design failure mode: a layout that requires removing a load-bearing wall. When KTR Builders handles your interior, Er. Sasi Kumar identifies load-bearing elements before the layout is planned. No surprises mid-project.

02

MEP Coordination Included

Electrical point placement, plumbing re-routes, ventilation positioning — all coordinated with the interior layout before walls are finished. No surface finish hides a poorly routed cable or drain.

03

Waterproofing Before Tile

Bathroom and kitchen waterproofing is an engineering decision, not a finishing step. The membrane is specified, installed, and tested before any tile goes in. This is how waterproofing should work.

"Most interior design failures happen at the interface between the designer and the builder. At KTR Builders, there is no interface — the same engineer handles both."

Interior Design Process — Mayiladuthurai

01

Site Visit & Space Assessment

Er. Sasi Kumar visits the Mayiladuthurai site. Structural assessment of existing space. Load-bearing wall identification. Existing electrical, plumbing, ventilation runs mapped. Natural light analysis. Floor plan measured and verified.

02

Space Planning & Layout

Room-by-room layout planning. Furniture placement, storage allocation, kitchen work triangle, bedroom ergonomics, bathroom fixture arrangement. Layout confirmed before any material is specified.

03

Material & Finish Specification

Flooring, wall finish, ceiling, kitchen shutters and countertop, bathroom tile — all specified for durability and maintenance practicality in Mayiladuthurai's climate conditions.

04

MEP & Joinery Coordination

Electrical point positions, lighting layout, plumbing routes, wardrobe dimensions, kitchen unit dimensions — all confirmed and documented before execution begins.

05

Execution & Handover

Installation supervised against agreed drawings. Key installation stages inspected. Final walkthrough against scope. Handover.
